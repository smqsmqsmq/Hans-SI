RESEARCH EXPERIENCE

The effect of confinement on the stability of planar dense wakes

Mphil. Thesis Project

9/2015-present

This experimental research focuses on investigating how the confinement ratio and length influence the stability in a planar dense wake consisting of a central stream of CO2 sandwiched by two identical outer streams of air, confined by solid walls of variable length which act as confinement.

Designed and manufactured the whole experimental setup, including testing section, pipeline system and measurement diagnostics system. Currently using HWA anemometer to measure the flow oscillation and the schlieren system to conduct flow visualization.

Current results: the dense wake tends to be more globally unstable when more confined both in terms of confinement ratio and confinement length.



CFD simulations on cavitation flow of cryogenic fluids

Undergraduate graduation thesis

12/2014-6/2015

CA Dynamic Cavitation Model (DCM) was applied to CFD software Ansys Fluent to analyze unsteady cryogenic fluid cavitation. This model considered the thermodynamic effect which can strongly affect cavitation process in cryogenic fluid. Simulation results were compared with previous experimental results to validate the new model.

Modified the DCM model, constructed calculating mesh domain and conducted CFD simulation in Ansys.
